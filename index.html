TASK 1: DIGITAL TECHNIQUES - Evidence of Understanding
Watch tutorial on enlarging details and enhancing images

Digital Enhancement Techniques Exploration

Key Digital Techniques

1
Enlarging Important Details

Learn how to select, isolate and enlarge specific details from artwork:

  • Use Selection tools (Marquee, Lasso, Object Selection)
  • Copy and paste selections to new layers
  • Resize and position details for emphasis
2
Adding Noise Filter Effects

Add texture and depth to your images using noise filters:

  • Apply Filter > Noise > Add Noise
  • Experiment with different amounts (5-15%)
  • Try both Uniform and Gaussian distributions
  • Use Monochromatic option for best results
3
Color Effects and Blending Modes

Enhance visual interest with color overlays and blending modes:

  • Create new layers filled with color
  • Experiment with blending modes (Multiply, Overlay, Screen)
  • Adjust opacity for subtle effects
  • Use color to create visual connections

TASK 2: REFINED RESEARCH PAGE - Applying Digital Techniques

Creating Your Refined Research Page

1
Select an Artist/Designer
  • Choose an artist or designer connected to your theme
  • Research their work, focusing on visual elements
  • Gather high-quality images of their work
  • Find relevant quotes or information about their practice
2
Analyze and Select Details
  • Identify important details in the artwork to highlight
  • Consider what makes these details significant
  • Plan how enlarged details will enhance understanding
  • Think about visual connections between elements
3
Apply Digital Techniques
  • Use techniques practiced in Task 1
  • Enlarge selected details for emphasis
  • Apply noise filters to enhance texture
  • Add color effects to create visual relationships
  • Ensure techniques enhance rather than distract from content
4
Proper Citation and Layout
  • Include proper Harvard referencing for all images
  • Format: Creator's Surname, Initial. (Year) Title of Work [Medium]
  • Add relevant quotes with citations
  • Create a well-balanced layout with clear hierarchy
  • Ensure all text is readable and appropriately sized

Harvard Referencing Guide Required

Why Proper Referencing Matters:
  • Shows academic integrity in your research
  • Gives appropriate credit to creators
  • Allows viewers to find your sources
  • Demonstrates professional research standards
Referencing Images

Format: Creator's Surname, Initial. (Year) Title of Work [Medium]

Examples:

  • Kusama, Y. (2016) Infinity Mirrors [Installation]
  • Sherman, C. (1981) Untitled #96 [Photograph]
  • Tate Gallery. (2020) Modern Movements [Exhibition]
Referencing Quotes

Format: "Quote text" (Surname, Year, p.X)

Examples:

  • "My art originates from hallucinations only I can see." (Kusama, 2002, p.23)
  • "Photography has always been about fiction, right from the beginning." (Sherman, 2003, p.117)

For online sources without page numbers, omit the page reference.

Design Considerations Tips

Visual Hierarchy

  • Make important elements larger
  • Use space to create focus points
  • Consider the viewing pathway
  • Ensure the eye follows logical sequence

Detail Selection

  • Choose details that reveal something significant
  • Enlarge elements that might be overlooked
  • Focus on textures, patterns, or techniques
  • Balance macro and full views

Visual Connections

  • Use color to link related elements
  • Create visual relationships between details
  • Align elements on invisible grid lines
  • Use consistent treatment for similar items
